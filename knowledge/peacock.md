## The Lie It Tells
The Peacock believes your worth is your output. It promises that if you just hit the next target, you'll finally feel secure. But the goalpost always moves. Last quarter's win is old news. There's always another metric, another PM doing better, another level to reach.

## How It Thinks It's Helping

## The Cost
The Peacock creates burnout and chronic dissatisfaction. You optimize for measurable impact over actual user value. You avoid ambitious bets because failure would be devastating. You can't enjoy wins because you're already anxious about the next one. Your identity collapses when metrics dip.
